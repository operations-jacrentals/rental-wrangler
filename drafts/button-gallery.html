Rental Wrangler — Button System Draft v1 · mockup, not the app

A proposed loudness ladder. The principle: visual weight = importance. Exactly one Primary per context; everything supporting it steps down a rung; destructive actions stay quiet until you mean them. Below is the whole vocabulary, every state shown at once, then a map of where each real app button lands — and the open calls for us to fight about.

The hierarchy at a glance

1 · Primary
The one commit/create action. Loudest thing on screen.
2b · Accent
Important but not THE action (soft-orange).
2 · Secondary
Supporting actions, dropdowns, neutral outline.
3 · Quiet
Exit / Cancel / Clear — low stakes, text only.
4 · Add
"+ Add X" empty-field affordance (dashed).
Side by side, weight reads top-to-bottom instantly — you always know what the app wants you to do next, and the quiet stuff doesn't shout.

Open calls — let's fight about these

A · Primary treatment

How much does the loudest button "pop"?

Flat
Pure fill. Calmest, most "spreadsheet-tool". Matches the current app.
my pick
Lifted
Fill + soft colored shadow + 1px hover rise. Reads as clearly clickable without being toy-like.
Gradient
Top-lit gradient + inset highlight. Most "premium", but can feel dated / heavier.

my pick
Border-warm only
Hover ≠ selected. Keeps the orange-fill meaning reserved for "selected/active".
Soft-orange fill on hover
More obvious it's hovered — but blurs the line with the Accent tier & selected toggles.

The rules (proposed)

  • One Primary per context. A card, a popup, a toolbar — each gets at most one solid-orange button. If two things feel primary, one is actually Accent or Secondary.
  • Hover never looks like selected. Selected/active = orange fill. Hover = border/surface warm only. They must never collide.
  • Destructive is never the loudest. Red stays quiet (text/ghost) until hover; solid red appears only inside a confirm.
  • Money & irreversible actions get a confirm step — Charge, Refund, Remove-with-payment, Admin override. Color alone is not the guard.
  • Icon-only ⇒ tooltip, always. And a ≥36px hit target (28px for hover-only card controls).
  • "+ Add X" is dashed, not a real button — it signals an empty field, not an action competing for attention.
  • Three sizes only (SM / MD / LG). No one-off heights.
  • Pills aren't buttons. Status/reference pills keep their own color system; don't restyle them into the button ladder.
Draft v1 — nothing here is in the app yet. Argue freely; I'll revise and we iterate before porting anything.
